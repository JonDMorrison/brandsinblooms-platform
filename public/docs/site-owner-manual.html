Dashboard Overview

Your dashboard is the control center for managing your online store. Here's everything you need to know to get started.

When you first log in, you'll see your dashboard with several key areas:

Statistics Cards

At the top of your dashboard, you'll find quick statistics showing:

  • Total Content - Number of pages and blog posts
  • Total Products - Items in your catalog
  • Categories - Product organization groups
  • Featured Products - Highlighted items on your site

Quick Actions

Below the statistics, you'll find buttons for common tasks:

  • Create New Page - Add content to your site
  • Add Products - Build your product catalog
  • Customize Design - Change colors, fonts, and layout
  • View Site - See your live website
üí°
Tip: Click "View Site" frequently to see how your changes look to customers.

First Steps Checklist

Follow these steps to get your online store up and running:

  1. Customize Your Design

    Go to Design and set your brand colors, fonts, and logo. This establishes your store's visual identity.

  2. Create Product Categories

    Navigate to Products ‚Üí Categories and create 5-10 categories to organize your products (e.g., "Indoor Plants", "Garden Tools").

  3. Add Your First Products

    Go to Products and click Add Product. Fill in details, upload images, and assign categories.

  4. Create Essential Pages

    Use Content to create About, Contact, and other pages. Edit your homepage to showcase your products.

  5. Set Up Payments

    Connect your Stripe account in Settings ‚Üí Payments to start accepting orders.

  6. Configure Your Domain

    Set up a custom domain in Settings ‚Üí Domains, or use your free subdomain (yourname.blooms.cc).

Pages Overview

Pages are the foundation of your website. Each page can have different layouts and sections to showcase your content.

Page Types

  • Landing Page - Your homepage or promotional pages
  • About Page - Tell your story
  • Contact Page - How customers reach you
  • Blog Post - Articles, news, or tips
  • Custom Page - Any other content

To view all your pages, go to Content in the sidebar. You'll see a list showing:

  • Page title
  • Content type (landing, about, blog, etc.)
  • Status (Published or Draft)
  • Last modified date

Creating Pages

There are two ways to create a new page:

Method 1: From Content Page

  1. Navigate to Content

    Click Content in the sidebar.

  2. Click Create New Page

    Click the Create New Page button at the top.

  3. Choose Page Type

    Select from Landing Page, About, Contact, Blog Post, or Custom.

  4. Enter Page Title

    Give your page a descriptive title. The URL slug will be generated automatically.

  5. Start Editing

    The visual editor will open automatically, ready for you to add content.

Method 2: From Dashboard

Click the Create New Page quick action button on the dashboard.

Editing Modes Explained

The Brands in Blooms platform offers three different ways to edit your content. Each mode is designed for different scenarios.

The Three Editing Modes

Mode Best For How to Access
Inline Editing Quick text changes on your live site Click text on your site when logged in
Dashboard Editor Major content changes, adding sections Dashboard ‚Üí Content ‚Üí Edit
Full Site Editor Editing while previewing on actual site View site ‚Üí Toggle edit mode

1. Inline Editing

Inline editing lets you make quick changes directly on your live site.

üí°
How it works: When you're logged in and viewing your site, simply click on any text to start editing. A floating toolbar appears with formatting options.

Features:

  • Click text to edit in place
  • Floating toolbar for bold, italic, headings, links, colors
  • Color picker for text customization
  • Link editor for buttons and text links
  • Auto-save every 2 seconds

When to use:

  • Fixing typos
  • Updating product descriptions
  • Changing headlines
  • Quick color adjustments

2. Dashboard Editor

The dashboard editor provides a complete 3-panel interface for major content changes.

Features:

  • Three-panel layout (sections, preview, settings)
  • Add/remove/reorder sections
  • Desktop/tablet/mobile preview modes
  • Section Settings panel for detailed customization
  • Image upload and management
  • Page metadata (title, slug, SEO)

When to use:

  • Creating new pages
  • Adding or removing sections
  • Major layout changes
  • Testing mobile responsiveness
  • SEO optimization

3. Full Site Editor

Edit your content while viewing it on the actual site with a top toolbar.

Features:

  • Edit on the real site (not a preview)
  • Toggle between Edit and Navigate modes
  • Section-level editing
  • Top toolbar with settings access
  • Auto-save with 2-second debounce

When to use:

  • Seeing exactly how customers see your site
  • Making section-level adjustments
  • Testing interactions and flows
  • Quick edits while browsing your site

Pro Tip: Use Dashboard Editor for big changes, Inline Editing for quick fixes, and Full Site Editor when you want to see exactly what customers see.

Image Management

Adding Products

Build your product catalog with detailed product information.

‚ÑπÔ∏è
Before Adding Products: Create at least one product category first. Products must be assigned to a category.

  1. Navigate to Products

    Click Products in the sidebar, then Add Product.

  2. Enter Product Name

    Give your product a clear, descriptive name.

  3. Set SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)

    SKU (Stock Keeping Unit) - A unique code that identifies your product for inventory tracking. Toggle "Auto-Generate SKU" ON for automatic generation, or enter your own unique code.

  4. Write Description

    Add detailed product information, care instructions, and benefits.

  5. Set Price

    Enter regular price. Optionally add a "Compare At" price to show savings.

  6. Assign Categories

    Select primary category (required) and any additional categories.

  7. Upload Images

    Add up to 10 product images. Drag to reorder - first image is the featured image.

  8. Set Inventory

    Enter current stock count. Set low stock alert threshold (default: 10).

  9. Mark as Featured (Optional)

    Toggle "Featured Product" to highlight this item in Featured Products sections.

  10. Activate and Save

    Toggle "Active on Site" to ON, then click Create Product.

‚ö†Ô∏è
Common Mistake: Forgetting to toggle "Active on Site" to ON. Your product won't appear on your store until you activate it!

  • Displays stock status on the products list

Stock Status Indicators

Status Meaning

To manually adjust stock:

  1. Click the product in your Products list
  2. Edit the "Current Stock" field
  3. Save changes

Updating Order Status

Keep customers informed by updating order status as you fulfill orders.

Order Status Flow

Status Meaning
Processing New order, not yet shipped
Shipped Package sent to customer
Delivered Customer received order
Cancelled Order cancelled (by you or customer)

How to Update Status

  1. Open the Order

    Click the order in your order list.

  2. Click Update Status

    Find the "Update Status" dropdown or button.

  3. Select New Status

    Choose the appropriate status (Shipped, Delivered, etc.).

  4. Add Tracking (Optional)

    For shipped orders, you can add a tracking number.

  5. Confirm

    Customer receives automatic email notification of status change.

‚ÑπÔ∏è
Auto-Refresh: The order list and statistics automatically refresh when you update an order status!

Email Notifications

Customers automatically receive email notifications when:

  • Order is placed (confirmation email)
  • Order status changes to Shipped
  • Order is delivered
  • Refund is processed

Typical Workflow

  1. New order arrives (Status: Processing, Payment: Paid)
  2. Prepare items from inventory
  3. Package order for shipping
  4. Update to Shipped (add tracking number)
  5. Mark Delivered when confirmed

Stripe Integration

Accept online payments securely through Stripe.

Why Stripe?

Stripe handles:

  • Credit and debit cards
  • Digital wallets (Apple Pay, Google Pay)
  • Fraud prevention
  • PCI compliance (security standards)
  • Tax calculation

Connecting Stripe (One-Time Setup)

  1. Go to Payment Settings

    Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Payments.

  2. Click Connect with Stripe

    You'll be redirected to Stripe's website.

  3. Create or Log In

    Either create a new Stripe account or log into an existing one.

  4. Complete Onboarding

    Provide business information, bank account details, and tax identification.

  5. Return to Dashboard

    You'll be redirected back with a "Connected" status.

‚ö†Ô∏è
Important: Complete all Stripe onboarding requirements before going live. Check your Payment Settings page for any missing information.

Testing Payments

Before accepting real payments, test your checkout:

  1. Add a product to cart on your live site
  2. Go to checkout
  3. Use Stripe test card: 4242 4242 4242 4242
  4. Enter any future expiration and any 3-digit CVC
  5. Complete checkout

Test orders appear in your Orders list with a "Test Mode" label.

Going Live

In your Stripe dashboard, toggle from "Test Mode" to "Live Mode". Your site now accepts real payments!

DNS Configuration

Add DNS records at your domain registrar to connect your domain.

DNS Records to Add

‚ÑπÔ∏è
Don't worry if this looks technical! Your domain registrar (like GoDaddy or Namecheap) has a support team that can help add these records. Just copy the values below and contact their support chat for assistance. 
A Record (for yourdomain.com):
Type: A
Name: @ (or leave blank)
Value: [IP address provided]
TTL: 3600

CNAME Record (for www.yourdomain.com):
Type: CNAME
Name: www
Value: [domain provided]
TTL: 3600

TXT Record (for verification):
Type: TXT
Name: @ (or leave blank)
Value: [verification code provided]
TTL: 3600

Where to Add Records

Registrar How to Access DNS

Namecheap Domain List ‚Üí Manage ‚Üí Advanced DNS
Google Domains Click domain ‚Üí DNS tab
Other Look for "DNS Settings" or "Name Servers"

Verifying Your Domain

  1. After adding DNS records, return to your dashboard
  2. Go to Settings ‚Üí Domains
  3. Click Verify Domain
  4. Wait for DNS propagation (can take 24-48 hours)

DNS Propagation: Don't worry if verification fails immediately. DNS changes can take 24-48 hours to propagate worldwide. Try again later!
